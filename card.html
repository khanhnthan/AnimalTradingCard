Siberian Husky

Two thick layers of soft silky red fur surround this creature to protect it from the elements, including extreme heat and freezing cold.

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus familiaris
  • Average Length: 48 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 12-14 years
  • Habitat: Originally from Siberia, Russia

A working dog from Siberia, Russia, the Siberian Husky was originally bred by the Chukchi people to help in their hunter-gatherer society during the cold season. It is a playful and energetic breed, and requires constant companionship, activity, and discipline.
